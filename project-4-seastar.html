Seastar Vineyard
& Winery

For the IDEA Program 3rd Year Professional Development course, students work with industry mentors to create comprehensive projects for their portfolios. This project was guided by Spencer Pidgeon, an illustrator and graphic designer with expertise in creating illustrations for branding initiatives.

Over the course of 6 weeks, Spencer mentored me in crafting illustrated label designs for 3 different wines from Sea Star Vineyards, a sustainability-focused vineyard and winery located on Pender Island. After the project was completed, I further developed the concept into a rebrand for the winery that targeted a younger audience who enjoy natural wine and care about the environmental impact.

Sea Star Vineyard’s goal is "to produce exquisite wines that express the terroir of our Gulf Island home. A vital component of this involves our commitment to preserving local ecological integrity and to actively give back to the coastal environment that gives our wines their unique character."

This project's creative objective was to create illustrations with an ethereal/dreamlike feel in an attempt to communicate the essence of the environmental focus in each wine through concept/feel rather than literal depiction.

Project Overview

Luna Maris


A sparkling white wine: its label needed to evoke moonlight, reflection, waves and consist of a blue and purple palette. In the final illustration, the moon appears both as a sparkling figure in the night sky and as a shimmering reflection upon the ocean water at dusk.

Project Deliverables

Intertidal


A rosé that evokes the seaside, floating, and the bizarre yet intricate life that dwells in the ocean's intertidal zones along the Gulf Island coastlines. Its main colour palette is pink and orange to reference the wine itself and to add an element of surprise and visual interest in an environment usually dominated by blues and greens.

Ortega


White wine that tastes like sunlight on a warm beach day; its floral notes naturally lends itself to an illustration evocative of flowers waving in the sun on a mildly windy summer's day. The colour predominantly needed to be green and yellow to reflect this.

Inspired by the island's coastline, the updated logo is hand-drawn to reflect the organic nature of the wine and terroir it emerges from. Socials posts are minimal in design to evoke the calming sensation of being beachside, while leaning on simplistic sans serif type and photography to enhance the classic, timeless, and calming vibe of Pender Island. The eco-conscious audience who will be inspired to buy this wine can further rep their loyalty with a variety of t-shirt designs, and of course, a tote bag.
